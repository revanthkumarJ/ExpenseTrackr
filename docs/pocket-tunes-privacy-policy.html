Pocket Tunes — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 July 2026
In short: Pocket Tunes has no accounts, no ads, and no user tracking. Your music, playlists, and trimmed clips never leave your device. The only data we receive is anonymous crash reports that help us fix bugs.

This Privacy Policy explains how the Pocket Tunes Android application ("Pocket Tunes", "the app", "we", "us") handles your information. By using the app, you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information we do not collect

Pocket Tunes has no user accounts and shows no advertising. We do not collect, store, or transmit your name, email, contacts, location, browsing activity, or any advertising identifiers. We never access, upload, or share your music files or playlists.

2. Crash reporting (the only data collected)

To keep the app stable, Pocket Tunes uses Firebase Crashlytics, a crash-reporting service provided by Google. When the app crashes or hits a serious error, Crashlytics automatically sends an anonymous report. A report may include:

  • the error type and stack trace;
  • your device model, manufacturer, and Android version;
  • the app version and build;
  • the state of the app at the time of the crash (e.g. available memory);
  • a randomly generated Crashlytics installation identifier and timestamps.

These reports do not contain your name, your music, your playlists, or any other personal content. This is the only reason the app connects to the internet, and the data is used solely to diagnose and fix problems. The information is processed by Google on our behalf; see Google's Firebase Privacy and Security documentation and the Google Privacy Policy.

3. Permissions we request and why

  • Audio / media access (READ_MEDIA_AUDIO): to find and play the music already on your device.
  • Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS): to show playback controls in the notification shade and on the lock screen.
  • Modify system settings (WRITE_SETTINGS): only when you choose to set a song as your ringtone, notification, or alarm sound.
  • Internet / network state (INTERNET, ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE): only to send the anonymous crash reports described above.

These permissions are used solely to make the app work and for no other purpose.

4. Where your content is stored

Everything you create stays on your device. Playlists are saved in the app's local database, and trimmed audio clips are saved into your device's standard Ringtones, Notifications, and Alarms folders. Uninstalling the app removes its local data (clips you have exported into your sound folders remain, as they become part of your device's media).

5. Data sharing and selling

We do not sell your data and do not share it with third parties, except for the anonymous crash diagnostics processed by Google (Firebase Crashlytics) as described in Section 2.

6. Data retention

Crash reports are retained by Firebase Crashlytics for a limited period (by default up to 90 days) and are then automatically deleted. Content stored on your device is retained until you delete it or uninstall the app.

7. Children's privacy

Pocket Tunes is a general-audience music player and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Security

Because the app keeps your content on your device and collects no personal information, there is very little data to expose. Crash reports are transmitted to Google over encrypted connections.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected here with a new "Last updated" date, and, where appropriate, noted in the app.
